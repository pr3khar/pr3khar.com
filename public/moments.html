Moments
Openhouse is a holistic after-school learning space for children aged 4 to 15 years with classes on academic and extra curricular topics of interest. It increased engagement by 225%* and above all, it brought a smile to everyone who saw it :)
Background
The gap
There was a sizeable gap in communication when it comes to discussing what children learn and experience at Openhouse and the information that is communicated to parents. While Openhouse tries it's best to provide a comprehensive education and learning experience to their students, parents may not always have a clear understanding of what their children are learning, how they are progressing, and what challenges they may be facing, which also leads to drop-offs after a few classes.
What is moments?
Moments, as the word means, is all about sharing the in-class moments of the children with their parents. It is designed to bridge the gap between what students do at Openhouse and the information communicated with parents. It will make parents feel more connected and excited as they see their children do and learn exciting things at Openhouse. We primarily aim to start with in-class pictures and videos , but down the line, aim to add other forms like text, stickers, milestones, etc.

References
Popular social media applications: Instagram, Facebook & LinkedIn
All our users have used popular social media applications like Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn and others. Hence, they are somewhat already accustomed to the kind of interactions they offer and the flow of information.

So, we took inspiration from the kind of interactions and information architecture these popular platforms offer and scaled them as per our needs and requirements.

teacher's side of moments
Important points to consider
A smooth and simple experience from clicking a moment to uploading and sharing it
The entire flow from clicking a moment to uploading it needs to be simple and smooth as teachers are expected to upload them within the class hours and for multiple children. On average, there are about 8 children per class, so it was quite essential to make it smooth and simple, so it doesn't disrupt the flow of the class.

So, we started with listing out the essential components that need to be considered or be acted upon while uploading that specific moment and that sort them based on priority and probability of usage.
Step 1
Clicking/shooting a moment
This was fairly straightforward, there are two possible ways for the teacher to click/shoot a moment, one is through the openhouse app and the other is through the native camera app and then upload it (useful when teacher uploads it post the class). One element was to design for the case when there multiple media files for a single moment while clicking them through the openhouse app.
Step 2
Should we ask for captions?
We conducted a small study internally to see if there's any noticeable preference towards having captions. The result was very much in favour of having captions. So, the next question was to figure out how to go about it. There were two options: Making teachers write them for each moment personally or make them choose from a pre-defined list.
We were concerned about the quality of the text written by the teacher accompanying the photo/video as uploading moments transforms into a daily activity. On average, there are about 8 children in a class, so a teacher is expected to share around 7-8 moments per class. Hence, it is only natural that with time, the quality might degrade.

But the problem with choosing from a pre-defined list was that it would defeat one of the core goals of moments by removing the layer of personal touch.

So, we decided to not go with it and analyse the user behaviour and in case we feel there is a need, introduce it then.

The common side
The feed
Who will see it?
We recognized the potential drawbacks of promoting unnecessary competition driven by likes and reactions. Consequently, we've made the decision that each moment will be shared exclusively with the respective parents. This choice aims to prioritize a focused, growth-oriented atmosphere where every child's progress is celebrated without the distractions of social validation metrics.

In case of teachers, we thought that sharing moments with all the teachers of the same category (like a moment of a child painting would be shown to all the art teachers) might act as motivation to keep at it because it might become a tedious task after a while.
What sort of interactions do we expect from the user?
We explored a lot of reactions with multiple variations, one with just likes, one with multiple reactions like "lovely", "amazing", "wow!", etc.

In the end settled on a gradual rollout method, where we decided to start with just a like reaction, and then introduce comments and reactions. Eventually, we thought the number of reactions to be a ticking number that could also excite the teachers to keep at it.
